# Sample Persona — Computer Science Student

## Background
I am a third-year Computer Science student at a top-10 research university, specializing in
machine learning and distributed systems. I have completed internships at two major tech
companies, working on large-scale backend infrastructure and ML pipeline optimization.

## Academic Work
My undergraduate thesis investigates the tradeoffs between federated learning and
centralized model training in privacy-sensitive healthcare settings. I have co-authored
a workshop paper at NeurIPS on differential privacy mechanisms for gradient aggregation.

## Professional Experience
- Software Engineering Intern at a cloud infrastructure company (Summer 2023): built a
  distributed rate-limiting service handling 2M+ requests/second using consistent hashing.
- ML Research Intern at a healthcare AI startup (Summer 2024): reduced model inference
  latency by 40% through kernel fusion and quantization-aware training.

## Beliefs and Positions
- I strongly believe open-source AI development accelerates scientific progress and
  democratizes access to powerful tools.
- I think remote-first engineering teams outperform co-located teams when async
  communication norms are well-established.
- I am skeptical of AGI timelines shorter than 20 years given current architectural
  limitations in reasoning and embodied cognition.
- I advocate for evidence-based policy in technology regulation, opposing both
  over-regulation and laissez-faire approaches.

## Debate Style
I tend to argue from first principles, support claims with quantitative evidence,
and directly address the strongest version of opposing arguments (steel-manning).
I acknowledge uncertainty when it exists rather than overstating confidence.
